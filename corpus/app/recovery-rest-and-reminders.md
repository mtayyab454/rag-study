# Recovery Weeks, Rest Notes, and Reminders

GymStart tries to keep you going for the long haul, which means nudging rest as much as work. Three gentle mechanisms handle this: recovery weeks, rest notes, and reminders. None of them ever block you from training.

## Recovery weeks (deload)

About every **seven sessions**, GymStart suggests a lighter recovery week. On a recovery session:

- Today's weights are shown roughly **20% lighter** (rounded to the nearest 5 lb) for that session only.
- Your stored progression is **not** changed — the lighter number is a display adjustment, so you don't lose any progress.
- A green banner on the Today screen explains it: "Recovery week: we've nudged today's weights a little lighter. Easy weeks are how you keep going."
- The session is tagged "Recovery" in your history.

When you finish a recovery session, the deload counter resets and the cycle starts again.

## The rest note

If you trained **very recently** (within the last ~20 hours), the Today screen shows a gentle rest note: "You trained recently — rest days are when you actually get stronger. Here anyway? Great, keep it comfortable." It's only a note — you can absolutely train again if you want to.

## Reminders

If you have **Gentle reminders** turned on (the default) and it's been a **few quiet days** (three or more) since your last session, the Today screen shows a friendly, non-pushy invitation: "It's been N days. No guilt — even two machines counts, whenever you're ready."

The reminder only appears if you've actually trained at least once, and only if reminders are enabled. You can switch reminders off entirely in **Settings → Gentle reminders**; the setting is described there as "A friendly note on the home screen after a few quiet days. Never pushy."

## The philosophy

GymStart never uses streaks as pressure and never guilt-trips you. Recovery weeks, rest notes, and reminders all exist to keep training sustainable — the app's stated view is that showing up for even a couple of machines is the whole job.
